HYDRABADI BIRYANI

The perfect blend of Mughlai and Hyderabadi cuisine, this delicacy becomes a must have for all Biryani fans when made with National Sindhi Biryani Mix.
It's spicy, indulgent and above all, a supreme classic from the family of Biryanis.

Ingridients:

  • Chicken 12 pieces 1 kg
  • Onion Golden Brown 2 large size
  • Yogurt 2 cups
  • White Cumin 1 tsp
  • Black Cumin ½ tsp
  • Red Chilli powder 1 tsp
  • Fresh Lemon 3-4
  • Crushed Cardamom 4-6
  • Saffron Color ¼ tsp
  • Salt to taste
  • Basmati Rice 1 kg
  • Ginger Garlic Paste 2 tbsp
  • Biryani Mix (ensure plums are separated) ½ packet
  • Bay Leaf 1 or 2
  • Whole Black Pepper 7-8
  • Cloves 4-6
  • Mint Leaves 1 bunch
  • Warm Milk 1 cup
  • Saffron (optional) 1 tsp
  • Cooking Oil As per requirement

Directions:

  1. Marinate 1 kg Chicken with 2 tbsp brown onion, 1 tbsp Ginger Garlic Paste, ½ tsp grated papaya, ½ pack Biryani Masala,
    1 tbsp Red Chili powder, mint leaves, 2 tbsp of lemon juice, salt, let it rest in the refrigerator for at least 1-2 hours.
  2. Soak rice well for at least 1 hour. Wash rice well and boil rice with black cumin, 2-3 cloves, 3-4 cardamom, 2-3 cinnamon sticks,
    4-6 black pepper cloves, 3-4 bay leaf and mint leaves. Once done, half drain them in colander and keep them aside.
  3. Cook marinated chicken on low flame with 1 cup of cooking oil till chicken pieces are tender and chicken is done. Once cooked set it aside.
    Generally, it takes around 8-10 minutes for chicken to become tender and cooked.
  4. Now take a deep pan, grease it with cooking oil and add half serving of steamed rice, top it up with cooked chicken,
    top chicken with mint leaves, brown onion and lemon juice and then layer it with other half of the rice on top.
    Garnish again with mint leaves, brown onion.
  5. Mix warm milk with saffron color and pour over the flavorful garnished rice and squeeze lemon juice on top and cover it with lid and
    let it simmer on low flame for 10 minutes at least.
  6. Once done mix it with quarter plate from all four sides and serve delicious Hyderabadi Biryani in a nice rice plate topped with some crisp brown onion
    and fresh mint leaves along with Yogurt raita and some Mixed pickles and salad.
